Why Buy a Pool Route?

Pool route ownership provides immediate, recurring revenue with strong margins in a recession-resistant industry. Here's why it's one of the best small business investments available.

Immediate Cash Flow

Start earning revenue from day one. Unlike starting a pool service business from scratch, you acquire an existing customer base with established billing and service schedules already in place.

Predictable Recurring Income

Pool service provides monthly recurring revenue that's stable and predictable. Most residential accounts stay for years, providing consistent income you can build a business around.

Lower Risk Than Startup

You're buying a business with proven revenue, known customer retention rates, and established service areas. The financial performance is documented — not projected.

Recession-Resistant Industry

Pool maintenance is an essential service. Homeowners with pools need regular maintenance regardless of economic conditions. This makes pool routes one of the most resilient service businesses you can own.

Scalable Business Model

Start with one route and grow. Add accounts organically, acquire additional routes, or hire technicians to expand capacity. Pool route businesses scale efficiently with proven operational systems.

Strong Profit Margins

Pool service businesses operate with healthy margins. Low overhead, minimal equipment costs relative to revenue, and recurring billing create a business model with strong cash flow characteristics.

Buyer FAQ

How much does a pool route cost?

Pool routes typically sell for 10-12 times the monthly service revenue. A route generating $10,000/month would typically sell for $100,000-$120,000. The exact multiple depends on account density, customer retention, payment history, and market demand.

Can I finance a pool route purchase?

Yes. Financing options include seller financing, SBA loans, equipment loans, and specialized lenders who work with route-based businesses. We can connect you with financing resources familiar with pool service acquisitions.

Do I need pool service experience?

No. Many successful route buyers start with no prior pool service experience. The seller provides hands-on training, and we can connect you with industry resources including certifications through IPSSA and PHTA.

How quickly can I start servicing accounts?

Most buyers begin servicing accounts within 1-2 weeks of closing. This allows time for training, equipment setup, customer introductions, and software configuration.

What are the ongoing costs of running a pool route?

Primary costs include chemicals, equipment maintenance, vehicle expenses, insurance, and software. Pool routes are known for strong profit margins — typical net margins for well-run routes range from 40-60% of revenue.

What size route should I buy?

For owner-operators, most buyers start with 40-80 accounts. If you plan to hire technicians, routes of 100+ accounts work well. We can help you determine the right size based on your goals, budget, and desired work schedule.

What happens if customers cancel after I buy?

Our agreements include account retention provisions to protect buyers. We work closely with both parties during the transition to minimize cancellations, and our approach to customer introductions is designed to maximize retention.

Is there a fee for buyers?

No. Our commission is paid by the seller. As a buyer, you pay no brokerage fees and receive full access to our due diligence documentation, negotiation support, and transition assistance.

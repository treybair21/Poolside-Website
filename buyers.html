Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a pool route cost?

Pool routes typically sell for 12 times the monthly service revenue. For example, a route generating $10,000/month in service revenue would sell for $120,000. The multiple depends on factors like account density, customer retention, and market demand.

Can I finance a pool route purchase?

While traditional bank financing for pool routes can be challenging, there are options available including seller financing, equipment loans, and specialized lenders who work with route-based businesses. We can connect you with financing resources.

What if I've never serviced pools before?

Many successful route buyers start with no pool service experience. The seller provides hands-on training, and we can connect you with additional educational resources. The technical skills are learnable—what matters most is customer service and business management.

How quickly can I start after purchase?

Most buyers begin servicing accounts within 1-2 weeks of closing. This allows time for training, equipment setup, and customer notifications. The seller typically helps with the first few service visits to ensure smooth handoff.

What are the ongoing costs of running a route?

Main costs include chemicals, equipment maintenance, vehicle expenses, and insurance. Pool routes are known for their strong profit margins compared to most service businesses.

What size route should I buy?

If you're starting as an owner-operator, most buyers begin with 40-80 accounts. This provides good income while still being manageable solo. If you plan to hire help from day one, larger routes of 100+ accounts can work well.
